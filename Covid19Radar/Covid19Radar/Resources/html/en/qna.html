Heads up!

If you have a cold or fever, refrain from going out, such as taking a break from school or work. You should measure and record your body temperature daily.

In the following cases, you should call the consultation center for people with potential exposure to COVID-19 set up at your nearest public health center.

  1. If you have had cold symptoms or a fever of 37.5°C or over for four days or more
  2. if you have a strong feeling of weariness (fatigue) or shortness of breath (difficulty breathing)

- In addition, if you have underlying diseases, are elderly people, are pregnant, consult with us if this condition continues for more than 2 days.
- If your symptoms do not reach this standard, please consult your GP or local medical institution.

Based on your telephone consultation, If you suspect you have an infection, we will make adjustments as necessary to ensure that you will see doctor.

At the moment, available evidence suggests transmission of COVID-19 occurs mostly via droplets which might be inhaled or touched by others.

  1. Infection by inhaling droplets occurs when an infected person coughs or sneezes.
  2. Infection by contact occurs when an infected person covers coughs or sneezes with their hands and touches objects around them with those hands.

Please do the following:

  1. Let's wash your hands with soap or alcohol-based hand sanitizer.
  2. Please wear a mask properly. In addition, let's thoroughly do “cough etiquette”.
  3. Elderly people and people with chronic illnesses should avoid public transportation and crowds.

Instead of receiving multiple medical treatment periods, please visit a medical institution introduced by the “Returnee / Contact Center” etc. (“Returnee / Contact Outpatient”, etc.). When visiting the clinic, wear a mask and wash your hands and cough etiquette thoroughly.
